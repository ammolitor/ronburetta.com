2019 IN REVIEW

In July 2019 CDA of East Central Missouri and our agency merged. Martin Hueckel of CDA has years of investigative accomplishments. This includes thirteen years of operating his agency in the St. Louis area. Before that, he spent fourteen years as a surveillance agent then as a case manager for a national investigative agency. Prior to that he served in the military where he attended the U.S. Army Military Police Academy and the Primary Leadership Academy. His integrity, work ethic and experience made him an ideal fit. On the legislative front this past year was good. There were no major roadblocks added in our fight against fraud and in retaining access to many records. A hiccup was the Missouri Driver’s License Bureau now requires more steps to obtain a copy of a person’s license photograph. This has slowed the turnaround time but keeping this resource was important.

In 2019 we had more offers than ever to work as a subcontractor for other agencies. Most of the requests involved surveillance assignments. Only a few offers were accepted. What was part of most proposals was a fee schedule sometimes less than what we pay our own employees. Someone is taking these cases based on a willingness to work cheap. Don’t be shy about inquiring who is handling your assignment, what are their credentials, are they licensed and if this involves sub-contractors.
